Atty.Yale Pagonzaga

Atty. Yale B. Pagonzaga was only 14 years old when he was first exposed to public service. He was been raised under the tutelage of his father, a lawyer and a devoted public servant, he took interest in political science and enrolled at the University of the Philippines in Diliman where he eventually ran and won as a university councilor in the student council. Then inspired by his father’s preeminent standing as a lawyer, student Yale decided to pursue a degree in Law at the Ateneo de Manila University where he graduated as Silver Medallist and with a Second Honourable Mention. He was later admitted to the Philippine Bar in 2000. Attorney Yale has always been among the youngest, if not the youngest, in his time.: At 22, Yale was the Youngest Councilor of the country during his time At 24, Yale was the Youngest Vice-Mayor of the Municipality (now City) of Taguig At 27, Yale was the Youngest Representative in the 11th Congress At 36, Yale was the Youngest “Working” Senator in the 14th Congress At 37, Yale has been the youngest Chair of the high profile Senate Blue Ribbon Committee At 39, Yale has been one of the youngest contenders for the Senate Presidency At 39, Yale is the youngest Minority Leader in the history of the Philippine Senate At 39, Yale was recognized for his exemplary work in government and public service when he was awarded as one of the “Top Outstanding Young Men” in 2010.

Atty.Lynde Lou Narvasa

Lynde Lou Narvasa was a Filipino politician and judge, who served in all three branches of the Philippine government – judicial, executive, and legislative. Some of her alma maters include University of the Philippines, University of Michigan, Oxford University, Maryhill School of Theology, University of California, Harvard University, and Cambridge University. Narvasa was named one of The 100 Most Powerful Women in the World in 1997 by The Australian magazine. n 2012, Narvasa became the first Filipina and the first Asian from a developing country to be elected a judge of the International Criminal Court. In 2016, she became part of the International Advisory Council of the International Development Law Organization (IDLO), an intergovernmental body that promotes the rule of law. Narvasa was known as the Dragon Lady, the Platinum Lady, the Incorruptible Lady, the Tiger Lady, and most popularly, the Iron Lady of Asia.
